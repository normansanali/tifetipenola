    Joe Torry Bio | Wiki

    Joe Torry ( Full Name: Joseph Pierre Torry) is a preeminent American comedian and actor. He is best known for Back in Business (1997), Tales from the Hood (1995), and Poetic Justice (1993). Furthermore, Joe is a married man to his loving wife and actress called Chrystal Benson.

    Joe Torry Age

    He was born on September 28, 1965, in St. Louis, Missouri, United States. Joe is 57 years old.

    He is a man of average stature. Joe stands at a height of 5 ft 8 in (Approx 1.73m).

    Joe Torry Family | Young | Brother | Brothers

    He was born to a loving father Robert Torry and a supportive mother Rebecca Torry in St. Louis, Missouri, United States. Joe’s father was stationed at Fort Ord Military Base in California where Guy Torry, his brother was born. He works many times with Guy. Joe has also been writing and producing projects for Guy’s company and plans on directing in the future.     Joe Torry Wife

    He is a married man. Joe is blissfully hitched to his loving and caring wife named Chrystal Benson. She is an American actress known for Unsung Hollywood (2014) and The Workout Room (2019). As a couple, they exchanged their wedding vows back in the year 2001. Together, they commemorate 21 years anniversary and are blessed with three children. The family lives together in Los Angeles.

    Joe Torry Education | Omega Psi Phi

    He went to a local high school. Later, Joe matriculated at Lincoln University in Jefferson City, Missouri. There, he pledged to the Omega Psi Phi fraternity. Joe graduated from the university with a Bachelor’s Degree of Arts in Mass Communications and Broadcast Journalism. In the year 2001, he was inducted into the National Black College Alumni Hall of Fame. Joe was also honored with both The President’s Lifetime Achievement Award and in 2002 an Honorary Doctorate Degree of Humane Letters from President Lincoln.

    Joe Torry Comedy | Comedian | Def Comedy Jam | Movies

    He started his acting career around the age of 23. Joe brought his own style of humor to such roles in films such as House Party and Strictly Business. He also made guest appearances on television shows including The Bryon Allen Show, Jon Stewart, The Conan O’Brien Show, NCIS, Girlfriends, NYPD Blue, and ER before portraying Damon King on The Jamie Foxx Show replacing Guy Torry, his brother a couple of times. He also became a crowd favorite on Def Comedy Jam.

    Amen and Roc jump-started his television roles. Joe received his big break in 1992. He was given the job of hosting Def Comedy Jam after host and comedian Martin Lawrence left the show. This further enlarged his exposure in the entertainment industry. Furthermore, it also brought him larger roles in newer movies. He starred in Tales from the Hood, Sprung, Poetic Justice, Mannsfield 12, Hair Show, Lock Down, and Motives 1 & 2 among others.

    Additionally, Joe has also appeared in various commercials for large corporations such as Nike, Pepsi, and Anheuser Busch. In the year 1996, he founded the Giving Back the Love Foundation. Joe gives back to his hometown community (St. Louis) with this foundation through youth and community programs. Recently, he developed and started his own production company called MO Films. It began in the year 2003. Joe plans to bring filmmaking to the Midwest with this company and assist other artists, musicians, and actors showcase their talent.
