His career started in 2000 and ended in 2013.

    DeShawn is the son of Darryl Stevenson, who never married DeShawn’s mother, and was forced to pay child support to her, until he died of cancer at the age of 36. Because of the unfortunate events in the family, DeShawn lived with his godparents in Easton, California. He went to Washington Union High School where he started playing basketball, and in his junior year they won state championship. As he got older, DeShawn became better and better and in his senior high school year committed to Kansas Jayhawks, Kansas University basketball team, but his SAT score rendered him ineligible for the University, so after averaging 30.4 points, 9.7 rebounds and 6.2 assists per game, in his final school year, which earned him McDonald’s All-American honors, he declared for the NBA 2000 draft, and was selected as the 23rd pick by the Utah Jazz.
