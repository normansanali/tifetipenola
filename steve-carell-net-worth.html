    Steve Carell | Movies and Television

    Steve Carell gained popularity from the show The Office.

    After that, he appeared in many television shows like Life’s Too Short, The Daily Show with Jon Stewart, The Morning Show, The Simpsons, and Space Force.

    Similarly, he later acted in different movies like The 40-Year-Old Virgin, Foxcatcher, The Big Short, Crazy Stupid Love, Battle of the Sexes, Little Miss Sunshine, The Way Way Back, and Despicable Me, among others.

    His role in the dramatic film Foxcather as a millionaire and convicted murderer, John Eleuthera du Pont, garnered him a Golden Globe nomination for Best Actor.

    Moreover, he played activist Steven Goldstein in gay rights drama Freeheld, followed by another biographical drama, The Big Short.

    Early in his performing career, Carell was a part of a touring children’s theater company.

    The Office (2005–2011)

    In 2005, Carell worked with NBC to star in The Office. Carell played the role of Michael Scott. However, the first season of The Office did not do well. Nonetheless, with the release of the second season, Carell got certified stardom.

    Carell won a Golden Globe for his role in The Office. He received six Primetime Emmy Award nominations for his work in the series from 2006–2011.

    On April 29, 2010, Carell left the show when his contract expired because he wanted to focus on his film career.
