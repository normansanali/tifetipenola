    David Gene “The Cobra” Parker (born June 9, 1951) is a former Major League Baseball outfielder who hails from the United States. He was named the National League MVP in 1978 and was a two-time hitting champion in his career. Parker became the first professional athlete to make an average of one million dollars per year after signing a 5-year, $5 million deal in January 1979. Parker was the first athlete to earn an average of one million dollars per year. Parker’s career statistics include 2712 hits, 339 home runs, 1493 runs batted in, and a lifetime batting average of.290. He also has a.290 career batting average. 

    During the early part of his career, Parker was also regarded as a reliable defensive outfielder who had a strong throwing arm. During the period 1975 to 1979, he ejected 72 runners, including 26 in 1977. In baseball, he was named to the All-Star team in 1977, 1979, 1980, 1981, 1985, 1986, and 1990. Parker’s defensive prowess and strong arm were on display at the 1979 All-Star Game when he threw out Jim Rice at third base and Angels catcher Brian Downing at home plate. Parker also had an RBI on a sacrifice fly, and he was voted the game’s most valuable player.
