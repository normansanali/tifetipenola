    His game has helped FSU maintain the top spot on the Atlantic coast. After their recent victory fans are looking on the web to find answers to queries like- what is Jordan Travis’s ethnicity and nationality?

    Jordan Travis’ Ethnicity and Nationality

    Jordan Travis is an American football player who is of mixed ethnicity, with his father being African American and his mother being Caucasian. Born to Tony and Tammy Travis on May 2, 2000, in West Palm Beach, Florida, Jordan Travis is an American national. Tony and Tammy have done their best to keep their lives private.

    Travis Family Background

    Jordan Travis comes from an athletic family. His father, Tony Travis, played college football at the University of Cincinnati and professionally in the Canadian Football League. Travis’s elder brother, Devon Travis, played baseball at Florida State University and was drafted by the Toronto Blue Jays in the 2016 MLB Draft. Moreover, his younger sister, Janae Travis, is also a track and field athlete at Benjamin High School.

    Not much is known about his mother, Tammy, although it is said that she is the family’s support system. When asked about her son’s achievements, Tammy said- “I can’t think of a bigger word than ‘proud, I’m just so happy for him. He’s worked for it. He’s earned it, and you always want the best for your children.”

    Jordan Travis’ Faith

    Jordan Travis is a Christian and has spoken about the importance of his faith in his life. He has mentioned that his faith helps him stay grounded and focused on his goals and provides him with strength and hope that’s unmatched.

    Jordan Travis’ NIL Deal

    Jordan Travis has signed a number of NIL deals with various companies, including Beats by Dre, Hey Dude, Leaf Trading Cards, NextGenCamps, Hotel Indigo, Tallahassee Ford, and others. The exact terms of his deals are not public, but On3 estimates that his NIL earnings are worth more than $1 million.

    One of the most lucrative deals among them is by Beats by Dre. He is one of the members of the inaugural Beats Elite class, which includes some of the top college football players in the country.

    Travis attended Palm Beach Central High School and The Benjamin School. He was a four-star recruit coming out of high school and was ranked the No. 24 dual-threat quarterback by 247Sports. Travis began his college career at Louisville, but he transferred to FSU after his freshman season when he only got to play in three games.

    So far, he has played four seasons with the Seminoles and has announced his return for a fifth season. In 2022, Travis led the Seminoles to a 10-3 record in the Cheez-It Bowl and was named the Cheez-It Bowl MVP.
