    Isabela Moner (born July 10, 2001) is an American actress, singer, model, and social media personality from Cleveland, Ohio. She is extremely into playing the lead role in the TV show “100 Things to Do Before High School” as CJ Martin.

    The actress officially debuted with a Broadway titled “Evita”. In the musical, she sang a song in Spanish with a very famous singer, Ricky Martin. In 2013, Moner first appeared in the film “The House That Jack Built” for a small role as Young Nadia.

    The following year, the artist played the recurring character “Jenny” on the television show “Growing Up Fisher.” Subsequently, she acted in projects like Middle School: The Worst Years of My Life, The Nut Job 2: Nutty by Nature, Splitting Adam, etc.

    In 2017, Isabela starred in a blockbuster movie “Transformers: The Last Knight” for one of the leading roles as Izabella. From 2014 to 2017, she provided her voice for an animated television series titled “Dora and Friends: Into the City!”.

    Her one of the most recent acting projects was in the movie “Dora and the Golden City”. It is a children’s adventure film based on the Dora series. James Bobin worked as the project’s director.

    Some of the other actors in Dora and the Golden City are Jeff Wahlberg (as Diego), Eugenio Derbez (as Alejandro Gutiérrez), Eva Longoria (as Elena), Michael Peña (as Cole), Adriana Barraza (as Valerie) Madeleine Madden ( like Sammy), etc.

    Early years, parents, siblings and childhood

    Dora Famed Star, Isabela Moner was born to her mom and dad on July 10, 2001 in Cleveland, Ohio, USA. Therefore, her age is 18 years old, as of 2019. The actress’s father, “ Patrick Moner”, works as a firefighter and his mother, “Katherine Moner”, is a housewife.

    Isabela has two siblings, that is, two brothers named Jared and Gyovanni Moner. Jared is an athlete and Gyovanni works as a musician.

    Education-wise, the actress graduated while she was a homeschooler from the Ohio Virtual Academy. In 2015, Isabela released her first major musical album called “Stopping Time” which was produced by Broadway Records.
