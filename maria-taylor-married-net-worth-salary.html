    ESPN's Maria Taylor was the victim of recently-fired radio host Dan McNeil, who made a bad comment about her outfits. McNeil compared Taylor's outfit to something that AVN (Adult Video News) awards show host would wear through a tweet.

    The tweet has been since-deleted from his account. However, Taylor made a backfire against the Chicago radio host with a statement, where she wrote:

    "Well Danny Dearest if you would like to continue making sexist comments about me…please bring your misogyny with you to the NBA Countdown doubleheader I’ll be hosting tomorrow night. Hey, ladies remember you can wear whatever you feel confident in!”

    Dan McNeil has since-been fired from the channel station.

    Maria Taylor's Net Worth And Salary

    Maria Taylor, who stands at the height of 6 feet and 2 inches tall, accumulates her net worth from her professional career as an ESPN's host and reporter. She has been serving at the channel for six years. She first joined ESPN as a college analyst and a reporter in 2014. Three years later, she started serving as a host of College GameDay Built by Home Depot and ABC Saturday Night Football in 2017.

    Besides that, she served as a host of NBA Countdown.

    Before joining ESPN, she was at SEC Network, where she hosted SEC Nation as a college football reporter. Also, she worked as the reporter of the NCAA Women's Basketball Selection Show, NCAA Women's Basketball Tournament Preview Show, and the NCAA Women's Basketball Tournament and Women's Volleyball National Championship for ESPN.
