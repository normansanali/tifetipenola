    Two tragic incidents occurred under the care of a grandmother, leading to charges of aggravated manslaughter against 65-year-old Tracey Nix.

    In one case, Uriel Schock died after being left unattended in an SUV on a hot day in Florida. Tracey Nix, a former school principal, admitted that she “just forgot” about the infant, leaving the child alone in the car for an entire afternoon.

    This heartbreaking event came just three months after Uriel’s brother, Ezra, drowned in a pond while unattended at Nix’s home.

    Describing her mother’s role in the tragic events, Kaila Nix stated she and her partner, Drew Schock, did not trust Tracey after the death of Ezra, and they never allowed their firstborn to stay with her.

    While Kaila loved her mother and wanted her in her life, the negligent incidents shattered their trust.

    Kaila expressed the emotional turmoil of the family, recounting her heartbreak when Ezra was taken off life support and died in her arms. She also shared the devastation of losing Uriel, who died in her mother’s care after being left unattended in a car.

    Despite the devastating news, Tracey’s attorney stated she would not be making public statements and that she was “totally devastated” by the deaths.
